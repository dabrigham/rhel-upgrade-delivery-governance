From Fragile Spreadsheet to Governed RHEL Upgrade Delivery

Enterprise upgrade planning was being coordinated through a complex tracker with assumptions spread across ownership, readiness, scheduling, change/task relationships, dependencies, and reporting.

Challenge

The workflow had outgrown what a manually maintained tracker could safely govern on its own. The goal was not to replace human judgment, but to make assumptions explicit and reviewable.

Approach

  • Clarify the operational problem and hidden assumptions.
  • Define schemas and validation gates for baseline records.
  • Model change and task relationships explicitly.
  • Route exceptions to human review instead of hiding them in manual interpretation.

Sanitized Visual Evidence

Raw screenshots are source evidence only. Public visuals must be cropped, recreated, or fully sanitized so no internal names, domains, workbook tabs, file paths, server names, application names, record identifiers, or private status labels remain.

The visuals in this web bundle are synthetic recreations based on enterprise workflow patterns. No production data is shown.

The synthetic demo demonstrates deterministic classification, explicit review reasons, and safe separation of ready records from exception records. No production adoption, measured time savings, or upgrade success-rate claims are made without separate measurement.
